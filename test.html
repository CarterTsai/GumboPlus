Raptors end home skid vs. winless Jazz

Long on youthful promise but short on established talent, the Utah Jazz figured they'd take some beatings this season. Tyler Hansbrough scored a season-high 23 points, DeMar DeRozan added 18 and the Toronto Raptors snapped a seven-game home losing streak against Utah, routing the winless Jazz 115-91 Saturday night. ''This is not fun,'' frustrated Jazz coach Tyrone Corbin said. ''I don't think we showed up with the intensity that we needed to compete.'' Jonas Valanciunas scored 14 points and Rudy Gay had 11 as the Raptors earned their first home victory over the Jazz since Dec. 22, 2004.
